That Time I Broke My Arm

Preface

I'd like to preface this story by noting the fact that I am in no way athletic. My body wasn't built for it.I knew that then and I still do now. But I like to have fun, and part of that is getting involved in sports, regardless of how bad I am.

Introduction

Once upon a time, back in sophomore year of high school, I was due for a rec basketball game. It was overall a bad week, since I had midterms, projects, and essays all due over the period of three or four days. As if that wasn't bad enough, my mother, who's super-superstitious warned me multiple times that my horoscope says I need to stay safe, as I have a possibility of getting into an accident. I am not superstitious, okay I am a little-stitious (shoutout to Micheal Scott), I took extra precautions during practice to play safe for the game. I even bought myself a pair of boots to make sure I don't slip on ice.

Climax

The day of the game, I had two midterms for subjects I absolutely hated, AP US History and AP Chemistry. I completely bombed US History, even though I stayed up all nigth studying for it. The lack of sleep had me be delusional, and since high school cafeterias rarely sell coffee, I had no way to regain some sanity. School ended, and I quickly changed to play the game. My head just wasn't in it. But surprisingly, I was doing pretty well. I got cocky. I forgot my mother's warning. I went up for a layup, and I got tripped by another kid who was trying to block my shot. I was about half a foot above the ground, while my body tilted, making it fall upside down. To protect head, I put my right arm up. I quickly black out for a second, and I realize something is wrong. Everyone's looking at me with disgust. I try getting up and my right arm isn't following. I look down and i realize my arm is bent the wrong way, like Harry's arm when he participates in quidditch for the second time.

Conclusion

Thankfully, the surgery to fix my arm went well, but I had to take a few days off since my arm was just killing me. The doctor had given me a bottle of Oxys, which my mom just threw away since they can be quite addictive. But not only was I in pain, I wasn't allowed to take any pain meds. And the worst part? No prescriptions were given, so I can't even go buy anything in a pharmacy. A few valuable lessons were learnt that day though:

  1. However unscientific a mothers instincts are, you just don't question it.
  2. Never get cocky, not even when you're just playing a rec basketball game
  3. Always ask for a copy of the prescription, you know, in case your mother throws your medicine away
